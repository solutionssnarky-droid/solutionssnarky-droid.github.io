Gemini's Glow-Up vs Copilot's Struggle Bus

Google's AI is now inside practically every app you already use. Microsoft's AI is… also there. Technically. Here's what actually changed, what it costs, and whether any of this deserves your attention.

📅 March 13, 2026 ⏱ 8 min read 🏷️ AI · Workplace · Google · Microsoft
TL;DR — the short version

Google Gemini is now baked into Gmail, Docs, Sheets, Slides, Drive, Meet, Chrome, and basically your entire digital life. Microsoft Copilot is technically everywhere too — but when people have a choice, only 8% pick it. Good news: the useful Gemini stuff is already included in your Google Workspace plan. The fancy stuff costs ₹1,700/month (AI Pro). Copilot? An extra ₹2,500/user/month on top of what you already pay. Yikes.

Let's be honest — AI announcements are now arriving faster than your WhatsApp group notifications. Every week, some tech giant announces a new "revolutionary" feature that's going to change how we work forever. Most of it is... fine.

But the Gemini rollout of early 2026 is actually worth paying attention to. Not because of the hype — but because if you're a Google Workspace user (and most of us are), some genuinely useful things have quietly landed in your apps. Let's break it down without the marketing fluff.

Section 02

What actually changed — the no-hype version 🔧

Here's a transparent look at the real changes that have rolled out. We're not repeating press releases. This is what you can actually go and use today (or soon).

🔵 Google Gemini — the real updates

📧
Gmail

The Gemini side panel is live. Summarises monster threads, drafts replies that don't sound robotic, and finds that email you've been digging for since October.

📄
Google Docs

"Help me write" generates a full first draft from a prompt. It can also match the style of a reference document — great if your manager has a very specific report format.

📊
Google Sheets

"Fill with Gemini" pulls data from your emails automatically — useful for tracking quotes, contacts, or any info that really should be in a spreadsheet already.

📽️
Google Slides

Describe the deck you need, Gemini builds it — matching your existing theme. Not magic, but it beats starting from a blank slide at 11pm.

📁
Google Drive

Natural language search now works across Drive, emails, and calendar together. "Find the Q3 budget Priya shared in October" — and it actually finds it.

🎥
Google Meet

Real-time speech translation rolls out to business plans, designed to preserve your tone, not just translate words. Big deal for cross-border teams.

💡
Also worth knowing AI Overviews in Search now show up in 60% of US queries, reaching 2 billion users globally. Google Assistant on your phone is being replaced by Gemini in 2026 — including Wear OS, Google TV, and Android Auto. And Chrome has a new Gemini side panel with a "auto browse" preview that can automate tasks without switching tabs.

🔷 Microsoft Copilot — what they're claiming

To be fair, Copilot has been updated. Auto-installed for all M365 enterprise users, embedded in Word, Excel, Outlook, and Teams. GitHub Copilot (the developer tool) continues to be genuinely excellent. Azure's private hosting is a real advantage for compliance-heavy industries.

The problem isn't the feature list — it's the execution. The integrations are inconsistent. The price is steep. And 92% of users choose something else when given a choice.

Section 03

The honest pros & cons 🎯

Google Gemini

✅ What's Good
  • Core features included in Workspace Business — no extra charge
  • Works natively inside apps you already use daily
  • Handles text, images, audio, code, and video in one model
  • 1M token context window — can process huge documents at once
  • Fastest growing platform — 157% traffic jump in 6 months
  • 2 billion people hit AI Overviews in Search (without even knowing)
❌ What's Not
  • Best features need AI Pro (₹1,700/mo) — free tier is limited
  • New Docs/Sheets/Slides features still in beta, slow rollout
  • English-first — other language support lags
  • Privacy concerns on free plans (data may train models)
  • Still third in voluntary paid subs — ChatGPT habit is sticky

Microsoft Copilot

✅ What's Good
  • GitHub Copilot is genuinely excellent — widely loved by developers
  • Native in Word, Excel, Outlook, Teams — zero extra install
  • Azure private hosting: great for banking, legal, govt compliance
  • 400M+ M365 commercial seats — unmatched distribution
❌ What's Not
  • ₹2,500/user/month ON TOP of existing M365 — very expensive
  • Only 8% of users choose Copilot when given options
  • CEO-level admission that integrations "don't really work"
  • 90% access, but voluntary daily use remains stubbornly low
  • 39% market share contraction in just 6 months

Section 05

So who should use what? 🤔

Skip the generic advice. Here's a straight answer based on your actual situation.

💼
You're on Google Workspace (any paid plan)

Open Gmail right now. Look for the Gemini side panel. Try "Help me write" in Docs this week. You're already paying for it — might as well use it. Start with summarising a long email thread. 10 seconds. You'll be converted.

👩‍💻
You write code for a living

GitHub Copilot. Genuinely. 90% of engineering teams use it for a reason. It's the one Microsoft AI product that earned its reputation without asterisks. Worth every rupee.

🏢
Your enterprise runs on Microsoft infrastructure (SharePoint, Teams, Azure)

Copilot M365 makes sense here — especially with Azure private hosting for data compliance. Banks, legal firms, and government-adjacent organisations benefit most from this setup.

🎓
You're a student or educator

Check if your institution has a Google for Education license — 14.5 million students access Gemini this way, often for free. Use it for research summaries, essay structure, citation organisation.

🧑‍💼
You want the full AI experience for ₹1,700/month

Google AI Pro: Gemini Advanced + new Workspace features in beta + 2TB storage. For most people who live in Google's ecosystem, this beats ChatGPT Plus on pure productivity value.

⚠️
Your company is evaluating AI tools with a big budget

Don't commit without a pilot. 74% of companies report no tangible AI value yet — because they bought licences without defining specific use cases. Pick one problem, test 60 days, measure, then decide.
