🧑
Fair point, but…
"I have EMIs, a family to support. I can't just gamble on the market bouncing back. What's the actual risk to me right now?"

That's the right question. Here's the honest answer:

85%Oil Imported

India imports over 85% of its crude oil. Every $10 rise in oil prices costs India an extra $15–20 billion a year and adds 0.35–0.5% to the current account deficit. Sustained crude above $100 is the real economic risk — not the geopolitical event itself.

−0.3%Op. Sindoor Impact

The Nifty 50 fell just 0.3% the day of Operation Sindoor in May 2025. The "India-Pakistan war = market crash" fear is historically not supported by data.

#1Biggest Risk

SEBI data consistently shows retail investor returns lag index returns by 2–5% annually — almost entirely because of panic selling during dips. You are the biggest risk to your own portfolio.

💡 Pro tip on SIPs during a dip

Consider increasing your SIP by 10–20% during this correction. You're buying quality assets at a discount — this is precisely what rupee-cost averaging is designed for. A ₹10,000 SIP during the dip buys more units than the same amount at market peak.

7 Things to Actually Do Right Now

The golden rules — in plain English

Forget complex strategies. Here are 7 clear, actionable things you can do this week:

1

Do NOT stop your SIP

Every time you skip a SIP in a down market, you miss buying cheap units. Over 10 years, investors who stay consistent with SIPs typically outperform lump-sum investors by 2–4%. This is non-negotiable.

2

Check your emergency fund first

You need 6 months of expenses in a liquid fund before anything else matters. If you don't have that, pause new equity investments and build it first. Period.

3

Treat this dip as a buying opportunity

Investors who added during the Ukraine war dip (−5%) were sitting on strong gains within 6 months. Deploy spare cash in tranches over 4–6 weeks. Don't try to time the exact bottom.

4

Add some gold if you haven't already

Gold ETF AUM at record ₹1,021 billion. Gold gained 66% in 2025. A 10–15% allocation takes 5 minutes to set up on Zerodha or Groww. SGBs are tax-free at maturity.

5

Watch crude oil, not war headlines

The real economic indicator for India is Brent Crude. Below $95–100: India handles it fine. Sustained above $110: revisit equity allocation. Every $10 rise = $15–20Bn extra import bill for India annually.

6

Avoid adding to mid- and small-caps for now

Nifty Midcap 150 dropped nearly 2x Nifty 50 during Operation Sindoor. Your existing SIPs? Keep them. But don't add new mid-cap exposure until things stabilise.

7

Put your phone down after checking once a week

Research shows retail investors who check portfolios daily during downturns are 3× more likely to sell at a loss than those who check weekly. Seriously. Log off.

Common Traps

"But my colleague said I should…"

A quick debunking of the advice you're probably hearing right now:

🚫 "Move everything to cash and wait."

India's CPI inflation runs at ~4–5%. Cash earns 3–4%. That's a guaranteed real-return loss. And when things calm down, markets recover fast — you'll buy back at higher prices than you sold.

🚫 "Buy HAL and BEL now — defence is booming!"

HAL's stock already reflects its ₹940Bn order book. Chasing a 20–30% event-driven surge is speculation, not investing. Use a defence thematic mutual fund with staggered entry instead.

🚫 "I'll invest once the situation is clearer."

There is never clarity. Investors who waited for clarity after the 2008 Mumbai attacks missed a 90% rally in 12 months. When the situation is "clear", markets have already priced it in.

🚫 "Let me put my emergency fund into this dip too."

No. Never. Your emergency fund is your lifeline — it must stay liquid and untouched, always. Mixing emergency funds with market investments is the fastest route to a forced sale at the worst possible time.
